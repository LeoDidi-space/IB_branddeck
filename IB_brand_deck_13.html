Pattern 01
Single Line Monogram

Illuminate Bridge · Brand System

Design Pattern 01 / 03
01 · Brand Story

Two arches.
One horizon.
One shared future.

The Single Line Monogram reduces I&B's philosophy to its most essential gesture — two arches drawn with quiet confidence, resting on a single shared line. The left arch, in deep navy, stands for one person. The right arch, in warm gold, stands for another. The line beneath them is not a base; it is a bridge.


This is not a complicated mark. It was never meant to be. The strength of Illuminate Bridge has always been in the clarity of its purpose — to connect people, illuminate the path between them, and move forward with sincerity.


Decades from now, this mark will read the same. Precise enough for a business card emboss. Confident enough to stand alone on a signboard. Warm enough to feel human.

"人と人の架け橋になる。
To become a bridge
between people."
人 · Human connection at the core
橋 · The bridge — where two become one
光 · Illuminate — warmth & clarity
挑戦 · Challenge & bold ambition
誠実 · Sincerity & lasting trust

05 · Usage Rules

How to use it,
and how not to.

Consistency builds recognition over time. These rules protect the integrity of the mark across every application.

✓ Clear Space
min. clear space = height × 0.5

Always maintain clear space equal to half the mark's height on all sides. Never crowd the logo with other elements.

✓ Minimum Size
24px digital
10mm print
Preferred

Below 24px digitally or 10mm in print, use text-only lockup to preserve legibility.

✗ Do Not Distort
Stretched
Rotated

Never stretch, rotate, or skew the mark. Always scale uniformly from a corner.

✗ Approved Colors Only
Wrong colors

Only use Navy + Gold (color), full Navy (1-color), or full White (reversed). No other combinations.

Pattern 02
Illuminate Rays

Illuminate Bridge · Brand System

Design Pattern 02 / 03
01 · Brand Story

Light that precedes
every connection.

The Illuminate Rays mark is built around a single act: the moment light radiates outward before a bridge is even built. The gold circle at the center is the source — a point of warmth, possibility, and human energy. From it, rays extend in every direction, growing softer as they reach further.


Below that light, the I&B wordmark in Georgia serif grounds the mark with weight and quiet confidence. The two elements — ethereal above, typographic below — mirror the duality at the heart of I&B: the vision and the execution, the light and the bridge.


This pattern foregrounds the Illuminate in Illuminate Bridge. Where Pattern 01 speaks of structure and connection, Pattern 02 speaks of the energy that makes connection worth seeking in the first place.

"光から始まる。
Everything begins
with illumination."
光 · Radiance — the source of all connection
温かみ · Warmth — gold as human energy
広がり · Expansion — rays that reach outward
落ち着き · Gravity — the serif wordmark below
誠実 · Honesty — simple, no artifice

05 · Usage Rules

The rays must
always radiate.

The graduated opacity of each ray is a core design rule — not a stylistic option. It encodes the physics of light and must never be made uniform.

✓ Preserve Ray Opacity Hierarchy
✓ Correct
✗ Flat opacity

Top ray: 90% · Diagonal rays: 70% · Horizontal rays: 50%. This hierarchy must be preserved in all applications, at all sizes.

✓ Icon Above Wordmark Only
✓ Correct
✗ Side-by-side

The icon always sits above and left-aligned with the wordmark. Never place the icon to the side of the text.

✗ Do Not Rotate the Rays
✓ Correct
✗ Rotated

The topmost ray always points directly upward. The mark is never rotated — its orientation encodes upward growth and aspiration.

✗ Approved Colors Only
✗ Wrong color

Rays and circle: gold (#C8963E → #E8C27A) only. Wordmark: navy on light, white on dark. Never recolor the mark in brand or campaign colors.

Pattern 03
人 · Hito Arch

Illuminate Bridge · Brand System

Design Pattern 03 / 03
01 · Brand Story

A single stroke.
A person. A bridge.
Everything at once.

The 人 (Hito) Arch is the most poetic of the three marks. A single kanji — 人, meaning "person" — is abstracted into a pure geometric form: two diverging lines rising from a shared gold point, set upon a grounding baseline.


The left leg descends in deep forest. The right leg flows downward in warm gold. The apex — that single circle of light — is the human being at the center of every connection I&B creates. Two paths. One origin. One ground to stand on.


This mark speaks in the language of Japanese visual culture without being literal. Any viewer — Japanese or international — will sense its human quality immediately. It is the most universal and enduring of the three patterns.

"人を中心に。
The person
at the center of everything."
人 · The kanji itself — person, humanity
光 · The gold apex — a point of light
二道 · Two paths diverging from one origin
地 · The baseline — shared ground, stability
誠実 · The simplest mark is the most honest

05 · Usage Rules

How to use it,
and how not to.

The asymmetry of the mark is intentional — the right leg is longer than the left. Never mirror or make it symmetrical.

✓ Preserve Asymmetry
✓ Correct
✗ Symmetric

The right leg is intentionally longer, mirroring the 人 kanji stroke. Never make the legs equal length or mirror the mark.

✓ Clear Space
clear space = height × 0.5

The legs extend to the edges of the bounding box. Clear space is measured from the outermost points — the leg ends and the apex dot.

✗ Do Not Distort
Stretched

Never stretch or squish the mark. The proportions of the 人 form are fixed. Always scale uniformly.

✗ Approved Colors Only

Left leg: Navy only. Right leg: Gold gradient only. On dark backgrounds, left leg turns white. Apex dot is always gold.
